# GTAS vs AGI vs ASI

The most important distinction is that **AGI and ASI focus on intelligence**, while **GTAS focuses on adaptation**.

Intelligence is only one component of adaptation.

A highly intelligent system can still fail if it cannot govern itself, coordinate resources, learn collectively, remain resilient, or evolve within changing environments.

# What is AGI?

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is generally defined as:

> An artificial system capable of performing most cognitive tasks at or above human level.

AGI capabilities typically include:

* Reasoning
* Learning
* Planning
* Problem Solving
* Language
* Tool Use
* Transfer Learning

AGI focuses on:

```text
Mind
```

rather than:

```text
System
```

---

## AGI Architecture

```text
Environment
      в†“
 Perception
      в†“
 Reasoning
      в†“
 Planning
      в†“
 Action
```

Key question:

> How can we build a machine that thinks like a human?

---

# What is ASI?

Artificial Super Intelligence (ASI) is usually defined as:

> Intelligence that significantly exceeds the best human minds across virtually all domains.

ASI could potentially outperform humans in:

* Science
* Engineering
* Strategy
* Creativity
* Governance
* Research

---

## ASI Focus

```text
Maximum Intelligence
```

rather than:

```text
Maximum Adaptability
```

An ASI may be extremely intelligent yet still operate inside a poorly designed civilization.

---

# What is GTAS?

GTAS asks a different question:

> What makes any system survive, adapt, evolve, and remain viable?

This includes:

* Humans
* AI
* Organizations
* Economies
* Nations
* Civilizations

GTAS studies:

```text
Adaptation
```

not merely:

```text
Intelligence
```
