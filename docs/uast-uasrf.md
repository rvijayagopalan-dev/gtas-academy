**Universal Adaptive Systems Theory (UAST / UASRF)** is not currently recognized as a single, universally accepted scientific theory. However, as a synthesis framework, it can be defined as a **meta-theory of adaptation** that unifies principles from:

* Systems Theory
* Cybernetics
* Complexity Science
* Information Theory
* Evolutionary Biology
* Artificial Intelligence
* Economics
* Network Science

The theory attempts to explain how **all adaptive systems—from atoms to civilizations—sense, learn, evolve, self-organize, and survive in changing environments.** It builds heavily on the principles of Complex Adaptive Systems (CAS), where systems consist of interacting agents that learn and adapt, generating emergent behavior. ([Wikipedia][1])

# Universal Adaptive Systems Theory Framework (UASRF)

## Core Axiom

> Every sustainable system exists because it can continuously adapt to environmental change faster than it degrades.

# Seven Universal Laws

## Law 1: Information Acquisition

Systems survive only if they can observe reality.

Examples:

* Cells sense chemical gradients
* Animals sense predators
* Companies sense markets
* Nations sense geopolitical threats
* AI systems sense data streams

Information becomes the raw material for adaptation. ([Wikipedia][2])

---

## Law 2: Feedback Regulation

All adaptive systems operate through feedback loops.

Examples:

* Thermostat
* Human nervous system
* Markets
* Ecosystems
* AI reinforcement learning

This derives directly from cybernetic principles.

---

## Law 3: Learning and Memory

Adaptive systems must preserve successful adaptations.

Examples:

| System       | Memory         |
| ------------ | -------------- |
| Cell         | DNA            |
| Organism     | Brain          |
| Enterprise   | Knowledge Base |
| Civilization | Culture        |
| AI           | Model Weights  |

---

## Law 4: Self-Organization

Order emerges without central control.

Examples:

* Ant colonies
* Immune systems
* Open source communities
* Cities
* Internet

Emergence is a defining property of complex adaptive systems. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

## Law 5: Evolution Through Variation and Selection

Adaptive systems continuously:

1. Generate alternatives
2. Test alternatives
3. Retain successful patterns

This is the fundamental mechanism behind:

* Biological evolution
* Scientific progress
* Innovation
* Machine learning

---

## Law 6: Resilience Through Diversity

Monocultures fail.

Systems become resilient through:

* Redundancy
* Diversity
* Decentralization
* Modularity

Examples:

* Ecosystems
* Financial systems
* Cloud architectures
* Civilizations

---

## Law 7: Co-Evolution

No system evolves independently.

Every adaptive system exists within an ecosystem of interacting systems. ([Massachusetts Institute of Technology][3])

Examples:

* Predator ↔ Prey
* Enterprise ↔ Market
* Nation ↔ World Order
* AI ↔ Human Society
