# 1. GTAS Manifesto

## Vision

Create a universal science of adaptation that explains how systems survive, learn, evolve, and thrive across all scales of reality.

---

## Mission

To establish a transdisciplinary framework integrating:

* Systems Theory
* Cybernetics
* Complexity Science
* Information Theory
* Evolutionary Theory
* Artificial Intelligence
* Economics
* Governance
* Civilization Studies

into a coherent discipline.

---

## Grand Challenge

> Design adaptive systems capable of sustaining humanity for 1,000+ years while continuously responding to technological, ecological, economic, and societal change.

---

## Core Principles

1. Reality is dynamic.
2. Adaptation is fundamental.
3. Information precedes intelligence.
4. Feedback drives learning.
5. Learning drives evolution.
6. Diversity drives resilience.
7. Coordination enables scale.
8. Governance enables sustainability.
9. Intelligence emerges from adaptation.
10. Survival requires continuous evolution.

---

# 2. GTAS Core Axioms

## Axiom 1: System Existence

Every system exists within an environment.

```text
System ⊂ Environment
```

---

## Axiom 2: Exchange

Every adaptive system exchanges:

```text
Matter
Energy
Information
```

with its environment.

---

## Axiom 3: Feedback

Every adaptive system contains feedback loops.

```text
Action → Outcome → Feedback → Adaptation
```

---

## Axiom 4: Information

Adaptation requires information.

```text
No Information
=
No Adaptation
```

---

## Axiom 5: Memory

Learning requires memory.

```text
Learning = Information + Memory
```

---

## Axiom 6: Adaptation

Adaptive systems modify behavior in response to feedback.

---

## Axiom 7: Evolution

Long-term adaptation occurs through:

```text
Variation
Selection
Retention
```

---

## Axiom 8: Resilience

Sustainable systems maintain viability under disturbance.

---

## Axiom 9: Governance

Large adaptive systems require coordination mechanisms.

---

## Axiom 10: Emergence

System-level behaviors emerge from interactions among components.

---

# 3. GTAS Ontology

## Core Entity Types

### System

Collection of interacting components.

---

### Agent

Entity capable of sensing, deciding, and acting.

Examples:

* Human
* AI Agent
* Organization
* Nation

---

### Environment

External context influencing the system.

---

### Information

Representation of state and change.

---

### Knowledge

Structured information enabling action.

---

### Memory

Persistent storage of adaptations.

---

### Decision

Selection among alternatives.

---

### Action

Execution of behavior.

---

### Feedback

Outcome information returned to the system.

---

### Adaptation

Behavioral modification.

---

### Learning

Accumulation of improved responses.

---

### Evolution

Long-term structural adaptation.

---

### Governance

Coordination and constraint mechanisms.

---

### Resilience

Capacity to recover and persist.

---

### Purpose

Desired future state.
